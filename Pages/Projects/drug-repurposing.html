Drug Repurposing for Opioid Addiction

Project Overview: This project explores gene expression and drug interaction data to find potential repurposed drugs for treating opioid addiction. The goal is to identify existing FDA-approved drugs that could serve as a treatment alternative based on molecular interactions and pathways.

A graphical representation of the data analysis used to identify potential repurposed drugs for opioid addiction.

Key Findings:

  • Identified several potential FDA-approved drugs that interact with opioid receptors.
  • Developed a database of gene expressions linked to addiction pathways.
  • Proposed new drug candidates for further clinical trials and research.

Methodology:

We used bioinformatics tools to analyze gene expression data, drug interaction databases, and molecular docking simulations to identify drugs that interact with opioid receptors. The analysis also involved literature review to find drugs with similar molecular profiles to opioids, followed by a computational screening.

Future Work:

This project is ongoing, with future steps including in-vitro testing of identified drug candidates and exploring new pathways for addiction treatments.
